Standard Equation of a Parabola
A parabola with focus at (a, 0) and directrix x = −a.
Definition: distance to focus = distance to directrix
📡 A satellite dish antenna has a parabolic shape. Signals arriving parallel to the axis reflect to the focus where the receiver is placed. The definition: every point P on the parabola is equidistant from the focus F and the directrix line.
Place focus at (a, 0) and directrix x = −a
📐 Choose coordinate axes so the focus F = (a, 0) and the directrix is x = −a. The distance from P(x,y) to F is √[(x−a)²+y²]. The distance to the directrix is |x−(−a)| = |x + a|.
Square both sides
🧮 Square both sides (both are non-negative): (x−a)² + y² = (x+a)².
Simplify to y² = 4ax
✅ Expand: x²−2ax+a²+y² = x²+2ax+a². Cancel x² and a² on both sides. Solve: y² = 4ax. This is the standard equation of a parabola opening to the right. For satellite dishes: the receiver is at the focus (a, 0) and the dish follows y² = 4ax!

Tangent to a Parabola
Condition for tangency: substitute y = mx + c into y² = 4ax and set discriminant = 0.
Substitute line into parabola
🎯 An architect is designing a parabolic arch and needs to find the tangent line at a point to calculate the support beam angle. Substitute the line equation y = mx + c into the parabola y² = 4ax.
Expand to quadratic in x
📐 Expand: m²x² + 2mcx + c² = 4ax. Rearrange: m²x² + 2(mc−2a)x + c² = 0. This is a quadratic in x.
Set discriminant = 0 for tangency
🧮 For tangency, the line must intersect at exactly one point. This means the quadratic has a double root: its discriminant D = b²−4ac must be zero.
Solve for c
✅ Expand: 4(m²c²−4amc+4a²) − 4m²c² = 0 → −16amc + 16a² = 0 → mc = a → c = a/m. The tangent equation in slope form: y = mx + a/m. This formula allows architects to find the support beam angle at any point on a parabolic arch!

Standard Equation of an Ellipse
An ellipse with foci at (±c, 0) where c² = a²−b².
Definition: sum of distances to foci = 2a
🌍 Johannes Kepler discovered that planets orbit the Sun in elliptical paths. The Sun is at one focus. The sum of distances from any point on the ellipse to the two foci is constant — the length of the major axis.
Set foci at (±c, 0) and point P(x, y)
📐 Place foci at F₁(−c, 0) and F₂(c, 0). For any point P(x,y) on the ellipse: √[(x+c)²+y²] + √[(x−c)²+y²] = 2a.
Isolate and square twice
🧮 Isolate one radical, square both sides, isolate the remaining radical, square again. After simplifying: (a²−c²)x² + a²y² = a²(a²−c²).
Let b² = a²−c² to get standard form
✅ Define b² = a²−c². Then b²x² + a²y² = a²b² → x²/a² + y²/b² = 1. For Earth’s orbit: a ≈ 149.6 million km, b ≈ 149.5 million km (nearly circular, e ≈ 0.017). Mars has e ≈ 0.093 — noticeably more elliptical!

a²=25, b²=9. c² = a²−b² = 25−9 = 16. c = 4
c² = a²−b²
