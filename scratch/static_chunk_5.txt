Theorem 10.5: Internally Touching Circles
If circles touch internally, distance equals difference of radii.

Theorem 10.7: Central Angle = 2 × Inscribed Angle
The central angle is double any inscribed angle subtended by the same arc.

Theorem 10.12: Opposite Angles of Cyclic Quadrilateral are Supplementary
The sum of opposite angles in a cyclic quadrilateral is 180°.
Draw cyclic quadrilateral ABCD
🔷 Draw a quadrilateral ABCD inscribed in a circle (all vertices on the circle).
Join centre O to vertices
Join O to A, B, C, D. Arc BCD subtends reflex ∠BOD at centre. Arc BAD subtends ∠BOD.
∠A = ½×(reflex ∠BOD) and ∠C = ½×(∠BOD). The two central angles sum to 360°.
Sum of opposite angles = 180°
∠A + ∠C = ½(360°) = 180°. Similarly, ∠B + ∠D = 180°. Opposite angles of a cyclic quadrilateral are supplementary!

Magnitude of a Complex Number
The magnitude of z = x + iy is the distance from the origin in the complex plane.
Let z = x + iy
Definition of magnitude
📏 The magnitude is defined as |z| = √(x² + y²). Since squares of real numbers are always ≥ 0: x² ≥ 0 and y² ≥ 0, so x² + y² ≥ 0. The square root of a non-negative number is also non-negative, therefore |z| ≥ 0.
If |z| = 0, then z = 0
🧮 Suppose |z| = 0. Then √(x² + y²) = 0 → x² + y² = 0. The sum of two squares can only be zero if both are zero: x = 0 AND y = 0. So z = 0 + 0i = 0.
