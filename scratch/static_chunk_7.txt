Factor Theorem
(x−c) is a factor of p(x) if and only if p(c) = 0.
Part 1: If (x−c) is a factor
🔍 You\
By Remainder Theorem
📐 If (x−c) is a factor, the division p(x) ÷ (x−c) leaves no remainder, so r = 0. By the Remainder Theorem, r = p(c). Therefore p(c) = 0.
Part 2: If p(c) = 0
🔄 Conversely, if p(c) = 0, then by the Remainder Theorem the remainder r = 0. Substituting r = 0: p(x) = (x−c)q(x). This proves (x−c) is a factor.

Fundamental Law of Trigonometry: cos(α−β)
The cosine of a difference equals the product of cosines plus product of sines.
Unit circle setup
Distance CD = AB
📐 The central angle between C and D is α−β. Construct angle AOB = α−β with A(1,0) and B(cos(α−β), sin(α−β)). Since the central angles are equal, chords CD and AB are equal.
Apply distance formula
🧮 Equating CD² = AB² and expanding both sides. On the left: cos²α + cos²β − 2cosαcosβ + sin²α + sin²β − 2sinαsinβ = 2 − 2(cosαcosβ + sinαsinβ). On the right: 1 − 2cos(α−β) + 1 = 2 − 2cos(α−β).
✅ Equating: 2 − 2(cosαcosβ + sinαsinβ) = 2 − 2cos(α−β). Cancel 2 and divide by −2: cos(α−β) = cosαcosβ + sinαsinβ. From this single identity flows ALL of trigonometry!

Trigonometric Identities (Double & Triple Angle)
Double-angle formulas derived from the fundamental law.
cos(−β) = cos β
🧭 Navigation: If you turn 30° clockwise vs 30° counterclockwise, the eastward component is the same! By the fundamental law with α=0: cos(−β) = cos 0 cos β + sin 0 sin β = cos β.
sin(α+β)
🔄 Using co-ratios: sin(α+β) = cos(π/2−(α+β)) = cos[(π/2−α)−β] = cos(π/2−α)cos β + sin(π/2−α)sin β = sin α cos β + cos α sin β.
Double-angle identities
🧮 Let β = α in sin(α+β): sin(2α) = sin α cos α + cos α sin α = 2 sin α cos α. For cos: cos(2α) = cos²α − sin²α = 2cos²α − 1 = 1 − 2sin²α.
Triple-angle for sin
🔊 Audio engineers use this: sin(3α) = sin(2α+α) = sin(2α)cos α + cos(2α)sin α = (2 sin α cos α)cos α + (1 − 2 sin²α)sin α = 2 sin α(1−sin²α) + sin α − 2 sin³α = 3 sin α − 4 sin³α. This generates the third harmonic in synthesizers!
