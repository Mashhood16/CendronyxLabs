Normal Form of a Line
Equation using perpendicular distance p from origin and inclination α.
Define the normal
🧭 Imagine a line L and a perpendicular from the origin O to L meeting at point C. The distance OC = p, and the angle this perpendicular makes with the x-axis is α.
Find intercepts
From right triangle OCA: cos α = p/OA → OA = p/cos α (x-intercept). From triangle OCB: sin α = p/OB → OB = p/sin α (y-intercept).
Apply Two-Intercept Form
Using x/a + y/b = 1: x/(p/cos α) + y/(p/sin α) = 1 → (x cos α)/p + (y sin α)/p = 1.
Multiply by p
x cos α + y sin α = p. The Normal Form! This is particularly useful in physics and engineering for finding distances from a point to a line.
