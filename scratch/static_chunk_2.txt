Exterior angle theorem
When these lines cross, they form a triangle with the x-axis. The exterior angle of this triangle equals the sum of the two opposite interior angles: α = β + θ.
Apply tan to both sides
θ = α − β. Take tan of both sides: tan θ = tan(α − β). Using the tangent subtraction formula: tan(α−β) = (tan α − tan β)/(1 + tan α × tan β).
Substitute slopes
Since tan α = m₂ and tan β = m₁: tan θ = (m₂ − m₁)/(1 + m₁m₂). Take absolute value for the acute angle. If tan θ = 0, lines are parallel. If 1 + m₁m₂ = 0, lines are perpendicular!
m₁ = 1, m₂ = 2. Find the acute angle between them.
tan θ = |(2−1)/(1+2)| = |1/3| = 0.333
tan θ = |(m₂−m₁)/(1+m₁m₂)|

Finding a Vector from Position Vectors
The vector AB equals the position vector of B minus the position vector of A.
Define position vectors
Use triangle law
To travel from O to B via A: OA + AB = OB. This is the triangle law of vector addition.
Rearrange
AB = OB − OA. The vector from A to B is the difference between the two position vectors.
Substitute coordinates
AB = (x₂−x₁, y₂−y₁). The components tell you the displacement in each direction. The magnitude |AB| = √((x₂−x₁)² + (y₂−y₁)²) gives the straight-line distance.

Law of Cosines
Generalizes Pythagoras for ANY triangle, not just right-angled ones.
Draw altitude AD ⟂ BC
🏔️ Drop a perpendicular AD from A to side BC, creating two right triangles.
Find CD = a − BD
CD = a − c \\cos β. The altitude splits the base into two segments.
Apply Pythagoras in right triangle ADC
b² = c² \\sin²β + a² + c² \\cos²β − 2ac \\cos β.
Simplify using \\sin²+\\cos²=1
b² = a² + c²(\\sin²β + \\cos²β) − 2ac \\cos β = a² + c² − 2ac \\cos β. When β = 90°, this becomes the Pythagorean Theorem!

a² = 5² + 6² − 2×5×6×cos 60° = 25+36−60×0.5 = 31
a² = b² + c² − 2bc cos α
Law of Sines
The ratio of a side to the sine of its opposite angle is constant.
Draw altitude h from B to AC
🌄 Drop a perpendicular from B to base AC. The altitude h can be expressed in two ways: h = c \\sin α and h = a \\sin γ.
Equate the expressions
Since both equal the same altitude: c \\sin α = a \\sin γ → a/\\sin α = c/\\sin γ.
Repeat for other sides
Repeat with other altitudes to get the complete Law of Sines.
a=7, α=50°, β=70°. Find side b.
b = 7 × sin(70°)/sin(50°) = 7×0.9397/0.7660
a/sin α = b/sin β
Law of Tangents
Relates side lengths to tangent of half angle sums and differences.
Start with Law of Sines
📐 From a/\\sin α = b/\\sin β, we get a/b = \\sin α/\\sin β.
Apply componendo-dividendo
Using componendo-dividendo: (a+b)/(a−b) = (\\sin α + \\sin β)/(\\sin α − \\sin β).
Apply sum-to-product
\\sin α + \\sin β = 2 \\sin[(α+β)/2] \\cos[(α−β)/2] and \\sin α − \\sin β = 2 \\cos[(α+β)/2] \\sin[(α−β)/2]. The ratio simplifies to the tangent form.

Half Angle Formula for Cosines
Express half-angle cosines directly in terms of side lengths.
Start with Law of Cosines
📊 \\cos α = (b²+c²−a²)/(2bc). This gives \\cos of the full angle in terms of side lengths.
Use double-angle identity
Since \\cos α = 2cos²(α/2) − 1, substitute: 2cos²(α/2) − 1 = (b²+c²−a²)/(2bc).
Solve for \\cos²(α/2)
After algebra using the semi-perimeter S = (a+b+c)/2, we get \\cos²(α/2) = S(S−a)/(bc).
Take square root
Taking the positive square root gives the half-angle cosine. This formula expresses a trigonometric ratio purely in terms of side lengths!
a=5, b=6, c=7. S=9. Find cos(α/2).
S=9, S−a=4, bc=42. cos(α/2) = √(9×4/42) = √(36/42)
cos(α/2) = √[S(S−a)/(bc)]

Express half-angle sines directly in terms of side lengths.

After algebraic manipulation using the semi-perimeter S, we get \\sin²(α/2) = (S−b)(S−c)/(bc).
